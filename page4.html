The books & music that colored my UW experience

Freshman year

Stuck in a dorm-room most of the time, this was the year that I discovered Gerry Cinnamon and indulged in nostalgic sounds from my childhood, like the Talking Heads. I also read a LOT (I actually thought I was going to be an English major for a decent amount of this year!), and my favorites were The Dispossessed by Ursula K. Le Guin, Kindred by Octavia Butler, the Red Mars trilogy by Kim Stanley Robinson, Persuasion by Jane Austen, and Never Let Me Go by Kazuo Ishiguro.

Sophomore year

This was my year of romance and soft beats. I got into historical romance (the Bridgerton series was my gateway book) and reality TV dating shows (...the duality of man is real). I also continued my science fiction tendencies, though, with Butler's Lilith's Brood series, some Philip K. Dick, and more Le Guin being among my favorites.

Junior year

This year I returned to my alternative favs and ventured out of fiction a little bit (!) to read genre-bending When We Cease to Understand the World by Benjamín Labatut, the time-bending play Arcadia by Tom Stoppard, and the biotechnology exposé For Blood and Money: Billionaires, Biotech, and the Quest for a Blockbuster Drug by Nathan Vardi. 

Senior year

Spotify says that I'm a music chameleon and I think this year that became more apparent than ever. I listened to a lot of German hip hop and indie music when I lived in Germany (my favorite artists were Dominick Hartz, Von Wegen Lisbeth, and AnnenMayKantereit). I also listened to a lot of British rap/hip hop (accidentally pre-empting my move to the UK!), like AJ Tracey, Dave, and Stormzy, and returned to British literature to read some more classics. A global year, for sure :)
