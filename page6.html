Suzzalo Library: Honors Biochemistry (BIOC 450)

Despite loving many different disciplines, as I advanced in my major coursework, I became increasingly drawn to the microscopic level of life. I was thrilled when I finally got to take advanced electives like immunology and virology during my junior year, but it was my 25-person Honors Biochemistry class in fall of my junior year that stands out the most to me looking back.

I loved this class because we got to read and discuss scientific articles frequently, deepening our understanding of biochemistry beyond that of a typical lecture course. I greatly enjoyed all of the writing assignments in the class, including midterm and final papers, as this was the first time I was prompted to actually critique a published article (a preview for the publishing peer review process!). 

One of my favorite assignments was to read and present a paper. I chose Costello et al.'s paper on probing the conformational dynamics of the SARS-CoV-2 spike protein using hydrogen-deuterium exchange and mass spectrometry (H/DX-MS) because I was fascinated by how we can use chemical instrumentation to probe biologically relevant mechanisms like viral protein conformations. I think about this paper and this class a lot and definitely think it biased me towards biochemical and single molecule ways of understanding the world. This scale of molecular structures and dynamics has the most explanatory power for me in understanding the big questions biology, and I hope to continue to work in this area in graduate school.
