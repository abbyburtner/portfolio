Honors Chemistry:
CHEM 1x5

Spring quarter of 2021 was a turning point for me. Despite the Zoom burnout after three quarters of online college, several things collided that quarter to remind me of my love of science: my awesome CHEM 165 professor, Professor Xiao, my very interesting history and philosophy of science class (PHIL 160), and the very cool scientists I got to meet at my weekly lab meetings. All of these things are too intermeshed in my brain to really parse them out, but I can honestly say that I ended that quarter with a much greater appreciation of science and scientists. Maybe it was getting to learn about the history of science--from alchemy to modern physics--that hooked me. Or it could have been Professor Xiao's awesome Art of Science assignment (see mine here). Or maybe it was hearing really smart people talk about things that they love on a weekly basis at my lab meetings that did it. All I know is that I'm so grateful for this quarter for reminding me why I'm doing what I'm doing and for inspiring me about what I might want to do in the future. For the first time, I could envision pursuing a PhD in STEM. After a really tough year, things finally started looking up with vaccines, sunny weather, good friends, and excitement for all the future science courses I get to take at UW.

Art in Science Assignment

The Art in Science assignment artifact features female X-ray crystallographers and their scientific "superpower" in a twist on Superman's X-ray vision. My idea for this came right after we learned about X-ray diffraction in my chemistry class and about gender's relationship with science in my philosophy class. At the same time, I was reading James Watson's The Double Helix: A Personal Account of the Discovery of the Structure of DNA and I was really shocked about his treatment of Rosalind Franklin (I'd heard the story before, but reading it firsthand is something else...). I had a lot of fun researching these cool women scientists and making this little drawing this quarter.
