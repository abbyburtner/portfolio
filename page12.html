Mary Gates Hall: Undergraduate Research Leader

Working internationally in Germany and in large, diverse labs at the UW opened my mind to how different perspectives can positively shape the production of scientific knowledge. It became apparent to me that being a scientist includes a responsibility to increase the diversity of perspectives in science. To this end, during my sophomore and junior years I volunteered as an outreach representative for the Office of Undergraduate Research at UW to speak on panels and with individuals about how they can get involved in undergraduate research. Interacting with undergraduate students, especially from underrepresented groups in research, and hearing their struggles accessing research and finding community within STEM fields at UW underscored for me the various inequities within access to research.

Life Sciences Building: Officer for the club Biology Students for Equity (BSE)

I have since tried to help foster more diverse and inclusive science communities by joining the club Biology Students for Equity as an officer during my junior year. In this position, I created a monthly journal club where undergraduates can present and discuss articles on equity/inclusion in science among peers (e.g., on creating anti-racist lab spaces). To help our club reach a wider audience, last year I also successfully led the application for a $3000 university seed grant centered on peer mentoring, the journal club, and informational panels meant to demystify barriers faced in STEM research and higher education. I am currently leading the 15-member officer team in carrying out the grant and throughout this process have learned that effective leadership is not just about executing new creative ideas but requires collaboration, patience, and determination. My various personal, academic, and scientific experiences have shaped the importance I place on the social side of science. Therefore, in graduate school and beyond, I aim to value and contribute to my community while pursuing research with the intent to discover new things and positively impact real people.
