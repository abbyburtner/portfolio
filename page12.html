Life Sciences Building:
Biology Students for Equity (BSE) club officer

A photo from the planeterium during our Art in Science Symposium in May 2024.

After getting involved in research outreach, I have also tried to help foster more diverse and inclusive science communities by joining the club Biology Students for Equity as an officer during my junior year. In this position, I created a monthly journal club where undergraduates can present and discuss articles on equity/inclusion in science among peers (e.g., on creating anti-racist lab spaces). To help our club reach a wider audience, last year I also successfully led the application for a $3000 university seed grant centered on peer mentoring, the journal club, and informational panels meant to demystify barriers faced in STEM research and higher education.

During my senior year, I led the 15-member officer team in carrying out the grant and throughout this process have learned that effective leadership is not just about executing new creative ideas but requires collaboration, patience, and determination. We culminated this year with a big Art in Science Competition and Symposium (photo at left) and I was excited to see how creative scientific communication through visual art might be able to reach more people to increase the accessibility of scientific information.

My various personal, academic, and scientific experiences have shaped the importance I place on the social side of science. Therefore, in graduate school and beyond, I aim to value and contribute to my community while pursuing research with the intent to discover new things and positively impact real people.
